How does recycling effect our environment?

Recycling can benefit our environment in many ways. It protects our wildlife, saves energy, and reduces the negatiave impacts that the extraction and processing of virgin materials has on the environment.

About

Throughout our education in grade school and highschool, we all learned the three “R's” for recycling: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle. When most people think of recycling, they think of the recycling plants which can sometimes hurt the environment in an effort to help it. A better way to be sustainable is to reduce the amount of waste being used and reuse products. You might be thinking, “ I thought this site was about recycling!” It is! Yet what most people don’t remember about recycling is it doesn't just come from using a recycling bin. Make an effort to create less waste. Reuse bags, jars, and boxes for multiple uses. Utilize old textiles for new products. Recycling not only saves the environment, but gives you an opportunity to be creative in how you create change. When I buy glass yogurt containers, I reuse them to plant flowers in. Using waste to make new things is a form of art. Recycling in all forms can bring beauty to our world. We just have to remember that there are so many ways to reduce, reuse, and recycle. Those were some of the basic facts of recycling, but how does recycling affect people and nature? Recycling helps preserve our environment and natural resources. There have been cases where people living near landfills have experienced health problems due to the pollution they generate. Some of the health problems that have been associated with living near landfills include respiratory problems, heart disease, and cancer. It is also important to note that landfills don't only affect humans, they also impact wildlife and ecosystems. Families should not have to relocate themselves because us humans do not recycle as much as we should. When we recycle, we reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills, which in turn reduces the amount of greenhouse gases that are released into the atmosphere. Additionally, recycling helps to conserve natural resources like trees, water, and minerals. For example, when we recycle paper, we save trees and reduce the amount of energy needed to produce new paper products. Similarly, when we recycle plastic, we reduce the amount of oil and natural gas that is needed to produce new plastic products. By recycling, we can create new products and minerals from old ones. Recycling helps to reduce the amount of pollution that is generated during the production of new products. Overall, recycling in general is important to protect the balance of life on earth as we know it. The effects might not be so obvious to us now, however they are relevant to us and do affect us both short and long term. Recycling is an essential part of protecting our planet and ensuring a sustainable future for generations to come.

Waste in landfills

This is an image of waste that gets dumped into a landfill. According to the article Recyling and Its Effects on the Environment, "...an estimated 85 percent of landfills are filled with unwanted waste" (Nodoushani, Stewart, Kaur 1). When waste ends up in a landfill, it can make hundereds or even thousands of years to decompose, releasing harmful greenhouse gases like methane and carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. These gases contribute to climate change and can have a negative impact on the envorionment. Landfills can contaminate soil and water, posing a risk to human health and wildlife. By recycling, we can help protect the environment, conserve natural resources, and support the economy.

Affects of Textile Recycling

When it comes to textile recycling, the classic recycling plants you think of are not the most beneficial. The recycling or reprocessing of textiles releases harmful emissions into the environment. Instead another form of recycling can be better for the environment, reuse. Reusing and recovering textiles for a new product is much more beneficial. Most people “often assume that textiles sent to recycling are wastes free of environmental burden,” yet, “ reused products and products made from recycled materials replace products made from virgin fibers.” Creating new textiles causes a lot of harm to the environment so reuse and recycling is much more worthwhile.

Plastic Bag Bales

Plastic bags are a mass produced product that tend to sit in landfills, about 500 billion plastic bags are used world wide yearly. With how frequently used plastic bags are used you would think that recycling them would be a priority and there would be systems in place for it to be easy. Unfortunately though, plastic bags are one of the most difficult and costly things to recycle. According to an article published by the County of Los Angeles it takes at least 300 years for plastic bags to photodegrade. Even when it does break down it is breaking up into micro plastics, contaminating the soil and water. Plastic bags are also in the top 12 most found items of pollution found on coastlines. They regularly are the cause of death for local marine life, either causing the animal to drown by getting caught in a bag, or having the animal mistake the bag as food, causing starvation. To fight the effects of plastic bag pollution, I try reusing bags. I reuse mine to clean my cat's litter box or line the small trash cans in my home so I'm not buying more. I also try to use my reusable bags when I go grocery shopping so I am not participating in the consumption of plastic bags.
